Science Topics!

Momentum- this refers to the quantity of motion that a object has. The term "Momentum" is commonly used in sports as sports require movement and momentum. In physics, the symbol for momentum is "p". when solving for momentum, the equation would be like this:

---> p = m ⋅ v

Impulse-In physics, it is a term that is used to describe or quantify the effect of force acting over time to change the momentum of an object. It's symbol is the big letter "J". this is usually Expressed as Newtom seconds or Kg m/s. When finding the impulse, your equation must look like this:

---> J = F⋅Δt

Valence electrons- this are the electrons on the outer most shell of an atom. An atom can give or take valence electrons depending on what other elements are they bonding with and how which one is more stable. For them become stable, they must first satisfy the octet rule or the duplet rule.
